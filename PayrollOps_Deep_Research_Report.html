02The Brutal Truth: What the Research Actually Shows

The payroll market is crowded. 15+ credible India payroll tools exist. greytHR alone has 20,000+ clients. No feature gap exists that a 3-person team can fill faster than well-funded incumbents.
Software is not the gap. Every major competitor has PF/ESI/PT/TDS auto-calculation, payslip generation, attendance integration, and statutory challan outputs. The gap is what happens after the company buys the software.
Implementation and operations are the gap. 66% of buyers encounter disruption after purchase. "Not one month has passed where we have not raised a ticket." — greytHR G2 review. Companies buy software, receive a login, and spend months trying to configure it correctly. Nobody does configuration as a service.
PayrollOps should start service-led. Setup + configuration + monthly support + custom reports + human contact. Frappe is the delivery engine, not the product. The client pays for operational excellence, not software subscriptions.
Full SaaS and global payroll are later. SaaS productization requires 18–24 months of service delivery to build reusable IP. Global expansion requires country-by-country compliance validation and is not realistic before Month 24.
Statutory filing ownership must be avoided initially. PayrollOps configures and validates. The client's CA files PF/ESI/TDS returns. This boundary protects against legal liability and acknowledges that PayrollOps does not yet have licensed compliance professionals on staff.

03PayrollOps Concept Definition

What PayrollOps Is

A payroll implementation and operations service powered by Frappe HR, designed for Indian companies that are either stuck in Excel or stuck with a payroll system that was never set up correctly. PayrollOps configures salary structures, builds custom payslips and reports, sets up approval workflows, creates CFO dashboards, verifies Labour Code 2025 compliance, and provides a named human contact who answers the phone during payroll week.

What PayrollOps Is Not

  • Not a statutory filing service — does not own PF/ESI/TDS filings legally
  • Not a replacement for your CA — the CA continues to own compliance filings
  • Not a pure SaaS product — you cannot "sign up and go live in 5 minutes"
  • Not an EOR — does not employ your people
  • Not a global payroll company — India-only for the first 24 months
  • Not a greytHR/Keka replacement based on feature count

How It Works

The client signs up for a Setup package. PayrollOps runs a discovery call, audits existing data, configures Frappe HR with correct salary structures and compliance settings, builds custom payslips and reports, runs test and parallel payrolls, trains the HR team, and hands over a working system. The client then subscribes to Monthly Support for ongoing validation, exception reporting, and human support during payroll week.

Who Uses It

HR managers at 50–150 employee Indian companies; founders who want payroll visibility without full outsourcing; CFOs who need department-level payroll cost reporting; companies migrating from Excel or from unhappy software relationships.

Division of Responsibilities

Client HR Team Does
  • Collects attendance, LOP, new joiner/exits monthly
  • Runs the payroll cycle in Frappe (one-click)
  • Reviews exception reports before finalizing
  • Distributes payslips to employees
  • Coordinates with CA on statutory filings
PayrollOps Team Does
  • Configures the system correctly from day one
  • Builds custom payslips, reports, dashboards
  • Verifies Labour Code 2025 compliance
  • Reviews pre-payroll checklist monthly
  • Flags exceptions before finalization
  • Resolves issues within 2–4 hours on payroll week
  • Documents the payroll SOP for client

Where Frappe Fits

Frappe HR is the delivery engine — the platform on which PayrollOps builds configurations. Its zero licensing cost, full customizability, and ERPNext accounting integration are competitive advantages. Its compliance automation gap is a responsibility that PayrollOps must fill manually with qualified expertise.

Why Market Size Is Not the Business Case

The India payroll SaaS segment already has 15+ credible competitors at ₹40–100 PEPM. Market size does not determine whether PayrollOps can acquire clients. The determining factors are: (1) whether the service gap is real, (2) whether target buyers will pay for it, and (3) whether PayrollOps can deliver at quality. The evidence says yes to all three — but only with a service-led model, not a SaaS-first model.

Key India Regulatory Context (Verified)

  • November 2025: All four Labour Codes took effect, consolidating 29 central labour laws
  • April 1, 2026: Income Tax Act 2025 replaced the 1961 Act; Form 16 → Form 130
  • 50% wages rule: Basic pay must be ≥50% of total CTC under Code on Wages
  • EPFO 3.0: API connectivity required for accurate ECR filing
  • Digital record-keeping: Mandated for all employers; 7-year retention
