03 / 35
Boundaries

What PayrollOps Is Not

Clear boundaries protect the team from scope creep, legal liability, and competitive fights we cannot win. These are strategic choices, not temporary limitations.

❌ Not Full Payroll Outsourcing
We do not own the payroll process. The client’s HR team runs payroll. We configure and support.
❌ Not Statutory Filing Ownership
We do not file PF/ESI/TDS returns. The client’s CA does. We configure the system correctly so filings are accurate.
❌ Not an EOR
We do not employ workers on behalf of clients. Deel/Remote own this space.
❌ Not Global Payroll (Yet)
India-first for 24 months. Each country needs separate compliance expertise we do not have yet.
❌ Not Feature Competition
We do not try to out-feature greytHR, Keka, Zoho, ADP, Gusto, Deel, or Rippling on feature count.
❌ Not Pure SaaS Initially
There is no “sign up and go live in 5 minutes.” PayrollOps is a setup + support service first.
Management Takeaway

These boundaries are not about what we aspire to. They are about what we can credibly deliver in Year 1. Violating them creates legal liability and competitive fights we will lose.

Process

How PayrollOps Works

A 10-step client journey from first conversation to monthly support. Each step is a defined deliverable.
1. Discovery Call
Understand current payroll process, tools, employee count, pain points, salary structure.
2. Data Collection
Employee master, salary structures, PF/ESI registrations, attendance method, leave policy.
3. Payroll Data Audit
Data quality report + Labour Code 50% wages check + salary structure gap analysis.
4. System Setup (Week 2–3)
Configure Frappe: company, employees, salary structures, PF/ESI/PT/TDS, workflows, approvals.
5. Dashboard & Report Building
Custom payslip template, Excel reports (salary sheet, bank file, cost summary), HR dashboard.
6. Test Payroll Run
Run test payroll for 5–10 employees. Validate PF, ESI, PT, TDS, LOP, net pay calculations.
7. Parallel Run
One full payroll cycle in both old system AND Frappe. Reconcile employee-by-employee.
8. HR Team Training
3 sessions: system overview, monthly payroll process, payslip generation + SOP document.
9. Go-Live
Client HR runs next payroll cycle with PayrollOps on standby. Go-live declaration signed.
10. Monthly Support
Pre-payroll checklist, exception report, post-payroll review call, issue resolution within 2–4 hours.
Management Takeaway

Timeline: 4–6 weeks from discovery to go-live. Every step is documented, repeatable, and measurable. The first 3 steps are risk-reduction; steps 4–7 are delivery; steps 8–10 are long-term value.

Productization

24-Month Productization Path

SaaS comes after service learning, not before. Productize the repetitive parts. Keep the human parts human.
Months 1–12: Service Foundation
Deliver 20+ implementations manually. Document every configuration pattern. Build internal “Payroll Config Library” — reusable templates by industry and size. Identify which parts take the most time.
Months 13–18: Internal Setup Wizard
Build an internal tool that automates repetitive setup: employee import templates, standard salary component library, PF/ESI formula presets. Not client-facing. Delivery efficiency tool only. Target: setup time from 4–6 weeks to 2–3 weeks.
Months 19–24: Client-Facing Layer
Build a PayrollOps client portal on top of Frappe: payroll input submission, checklist tracking, exception report viewing, payslip download, support logging. This is the beginning of SaaS — built on 24 months of real workflow knowledge.
Month 24+: SaaS Transition Option
If portal + Config Library are mature: Option A — license portal to other Frappe partners. Option B — self-serve lite tier. Option C — raise investment to scale. Only if service business has generated reusable IP.
⚠ Critical Rule: Do not build SaaS before delivering 20+ implementations.
The product must emerge from service knowledge. Building it before service learning = building the wrong product.
Management Takeaway

Productization is a 24-month path, not a 6-month sprint. The internal wizard comes first. The client portal comes second. Self-serve SaaS comes third — only after real workflow knowledge is captured.

Kill Criteria

Kill Criteria

These are the conditions under which we stop and redesign — not push forward with a broken model.
🔴 Pilots Fail Clean Payroll
If fewer than 2 of 5 pilot clients run a clean payroll in the first supported cycle, the implementation process needs fundamental redesign before any further clients.
🔴 Buyers Reject Pricing
If fewer than 3 of 20 interviewees express interest at stated pricing, revisit pricing or offer structure. Do not force a price the market rejects.
🔴 Support SLA Breaks
If average resolution time exceeds 4 hours on payroll week consistently across pilots, stop taking new clients until support capacity is added.
🔴 Frappe Cannot Configure
If Frappe HR v16 cannot support required India payroll configuration within 2 weeks of testing, delay client onboarding until resolved or pivot strategy.
🔴 Compliance Advisor Flags Risk
If a qualified CA or payroll compliance advisor flags PayrollOps delivery as legally unsafe, halt client delivery until compliance structure is corrected.
🔴 No Recurring Conversion
If fewer than 50% of pilot clients convert to ongoing Monthly Support after the pilot period, the value proposition needs revision before scaling.
“Killing the current approach is not failure. Scaling a broken model is failure.”
Management Takeaway

These are not pessimistic. They are guardrails. Every startup that scaled a broken model wished they had stopped to fix it. Review kill criteria at Day 30, Day 60, and Day 90.
